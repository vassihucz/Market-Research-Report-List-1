<p><p>Vaccine markets can be categorized based on the diseases they target. The Varicella, Influenza, Polio, Hepatitis A, Rabies, BCG, Hepatitis B, Pertussis, Diphtheria, Tetanus, and Pneumococcal vaccines each serve unique purposes. Varicella prevents chickenpox, influenza targets seasonal flu, and polio immunizes against poliomyelitis. Hepatitis A and B vaccines guard against liver infections, while Rabies vaccines are crucial for animal bite exposures. BCG protects against tuberculosis, and the combination of Pertussis, Diphtheria, and Tetanus (DPT) vaccines offers protection against three serious diseases. The pneumococcal vaccine helps prevent pneumonia and related infections. Other vaccines include those for less common diseases, expanding overall public health protection.</p></p>
