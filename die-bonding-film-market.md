<p><p>The die bonding film market is categorized into non-conductive and conductive types, each serving distinct applications in electronics manufacturing. Non-conductive types are primarily used for die attachment where electrical insulation is essential, providing high thermal performance without electrical conductivity. In contrast, conductive types facilitate electrical connections between the die and substrate, enhancing signal integrity and thermal management. Both types are crucial for ensuring reliability and performance in semiconductor packaging, catering to varying needs in the electronics sector.</p></p>
