<p><p>Telemetric devices encompass two main market types: wired and wireless. Wired telemetric devices use physical connections, such as cables, to transmit data between sensors and monitoring systems. They are often preferred for their reliability and low interference but can be limited by installation complexity. In contrast, wireless telemetric devices utilize radio signals or other wireless technologies to transmit data, offering greater flexibility and ease of installation. They are ideal for remote monitoring, but may face challenges like signal interference and battery life concerns.</p></p>
