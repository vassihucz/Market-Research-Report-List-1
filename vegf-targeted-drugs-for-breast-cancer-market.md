<p><p>VEGF-targeted drugs for breast cancer aim to inhibit angiogenesis, a critical process for tumor growth. Bevacizumab is an antibody that blocks VEGF, preventing blood vessel formation. Sorafenib is a multikinase inhibitor that disrupts tumor-associated angiogenesis. Ramucirumab also targets VEGF receptors, inhibiting their activation. Sunitinib, another multikinase inhibitor, affects multiple pathways involved in angiogenesis. Apatinib specifically inhibits VEGFR-2, effectively blocking tumor blood supply. These drugs are key in enhancing treatment options and improving outcomes for breast cancer patients.</p></p>
