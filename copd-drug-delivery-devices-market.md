<p><p>The COPD drug delivery devices market primarily encompasses Metered Dose Inhalers (MDIs) and Dry Powder Inhalers (DPIs). MDIs utilize a propellant to deliver a specific dose of medication in aerosol form, allowing for quick absorption in the lungs. In contrast, DPIs rely on the patient's inhalation effort to disperse powdered medication, offering ease of use without propellants. Both devices aim to improve medication adherence and optimize therapeutic outcomes in managing chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).</p></p>
