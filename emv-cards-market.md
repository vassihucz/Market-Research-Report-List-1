<p><p>The EMV cards market is primarily divided into two types: contact-based EMV cards and contactless EMV cards. Contact-based EMV cards require physical insertion into a card reader, utilizing a chip to enhance security and facilitate transactions. In contrast, contactless EMV cards utilize Near Field Communication (NFC) technology, allowing users to make quick transactions by simply waving the card near a compatible terminal. Both types prioritize secure payment methods, catering to consumer preferences for convenience and enhanced security in financial transactions.</p></p>
